The World’s First 3D-Printed Watch by Apiar

Date: August 2025

Apiar introduces Gen1.0, the first fully 3D-printed titanium watch, combining cutting-edge technology with sustainability.

Read more Apiar has unveiled Gen1.0, the world’s first fully 3D-printed titanium watch, marking a new era in horology. The brand uses advanced additive manufacturing, producing cases and dials with minimal waste — only about 14 grams of leftover material. The 39 mm case is paired with a high-quality mechanical movement from La Joux-Perret, ensuring both innovation and traditional watchmaking precision. Priced at around £2,250 (including VAT), the Gen1.0 sets a benchmark for sustainable production and design freedom in the watch industry.

Watches & Wonders 2025: Key Trends in Modern Watchmaking

Date: August 2025

The largest watch fair of the year showcased innovation, compact designs, and bold use of colors.

Read more Watches & Wonders 2025 gathered over 60 brands, making it the biggest edition yet. This year’s highlights include ultra-precise 5 Hz mechanical movements presented by Rolex, Grand Seiko, Parmigiani, and Chopard. Another trend is compact cases — Rolex introduced the Land-Dweller, and brands like TAG Heuer, Cartier, and Chopard followed with smaller, more wearable designs. Color took center stage: pastel shades, candy-tone dials, and warm gold hues such as “Honeygold” dominated the show. At the high end, Patek Philippe impressed with its Quadruple Complication, while Vacheron Constantin unveiled the Solaria Ultra. Overall, the exhibition showed how watchmaking combines technical mastery with playful creativity.
